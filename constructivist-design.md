## Overview
Constructivism (1915–30s) declared the artist an engineer. Language: aggressive geometry, diagonal lines, sans-serif slash. Compositions are dynamic fractures, propaganda made functional.

## Color System
Political palette: soviet red, international blue, mustard sunbeam, black ink. Only five colors; no gradients.

## Typography
Massive aggressive type. Kross (slant extreme), Teko (condensed industrial). All headlines italicized; extreme scale: 5rem vs 1.25rem.

## Layout
Governed by diagonal tension (`-10deg` skew). Grid fractured into triangles and trapezoids. Negative space as counterweights.

## Visual Elements
Red banner backgrounds, diagonal outlines, geometric icons (gears, factories), construction lines floating.

## Borders
2–3px solid black. Sometimes double. No rounding.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do skew every container by -10deg
- Do type set to extreme sizes
- Do use diagonal lines through imagery
- Do let elements overflow their frame
- Do embrace asymmetry as struggle
- Do use red / black base exclusively
- Don't round corners — industrial
- Don't use subtle spacing
- Don't animate — dramatic stasis only
- Don't use more than 2 typefaces
